💫 Grid & Flexbox Layout - Study Notes

🛤️ Direction (row & column)

  • row ➡️ Items lay out left to right.
  • row-reverse ⬅️ Right to left.
  • column ⬆️ Top to bottom.
  • column-reverse ⬇️ Bottom to top.

<Grid container direction="row">

⚖️ justifyContent (Main Axis)

  • flex-start 🏁 Align at start.
  • center 🔘 Center items.
  • flex-end 🏁 Align at end.
  • space-between ↔️ Even spaces between.
  • space-around 🔄 Space around items.
  • space-evenly 📏 Equal gaps everywhere.

Use for horizontal (row) or vertical (column) spacing.

↕️ alignItems (Cross Axis)

  • flex-start ↖️ Align at top/left.
  • center 🔘 Center along cross axis.
  • flex-end ↘️ Align at bottom/right.
  • stretch 📏 Fill available space.
  • baseline 📋 Align text baselines.

🔀 Auto Layout

Set xs="auto" on items to share space equally:

<Grid item xs="auto">Auto</Grid>

Combine with fixed spans for mix of sizes.

🧩 Nested Grids

You can be both container and item:

<Grid container>
  <Grid item container>
    <Grid item>Nested</Grid>
  </Grid>
</Grid>

This helps build complex layouts easily!

📐 Footer Example Layout

  • 👉 Outer container direction="row": columns left-to-right.
  • 👉 Inner columns container direction="column": links top-to-bottom.
  • 👉 Items: each link is an item.

🎯 Practice Tips

  • 🔍 Play with the Material UI demo to see effects live.
  • 📱 Test on different screen sizes to understand breakpoints.
  • 🚀 Use nested grids for real-world layouts like headers & footers.
