🦶 Website Footer Setup - Study Notes

🔍 What Is a Footer?

A footer sits at the bottom of a website and usually has links to every page, plus extra info or icons. It stays visible at the bottom, just like a header stays at the top!

🎨 Footer Design Elements

  • ➖ A black slash image (matches the header logo).
  • 📋 A column of page links (About, Services, Contact, etc.).
  • 🔗 Social media icons on the right (open apps on mobile!).

⚙️ Step 1: Basic <footer> Element

1️⃣ Add the HTML <footer> tag to your page.
2️⃣ Inside it, place a container div for your content.

✨ Step 2: Style & Add the Slash

🎨 Use CSS to give your footer a background and padding.
➕ Add the slash image on the left using an <img> tag.

🔲 Step 3: Lay Out Links with Grid

📐 Import Material UI’s Grid component.
2️⃣ Create a grid container and place each link in its own grid item so they stack in a neat column.

📱 Step 4: Add Social Icons

🔗 Use Material UI icons (Facebook, Twitter, etc.) in a grid item on the right.
⚙️ Make them clickable links that open the correct app on mobile.

📐 Step 5: Make Footer Responsive

🤫 On small screens, hide the link list and show only social icons.
🛠️ Use Material UI’s Hidden component to hide items at sm and below.

🎉 Final Tips

  • ✅ Test resizing the window to see your footer adapt.
  • ✅ Check console for any errors after changes.
  • 🚀 You now have a clean, responsive footer with links and social icons!
