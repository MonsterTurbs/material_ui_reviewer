1. Make the File 📁

Create a new file Revolution.js inside your components folder. This will hold your Revolution page code. 😊

2. Import Stuff 📥

At the top of Revolution.js, import React, makeStyles, useTheme, Grid, and Typography from Material‑UI. This gives you the tools to build and style. 🛠️

3. Setup Styles & Component 🎨

Use makeStyles(theme => ({ ... })) to define your useStyles hook. Export a function component Revolution, get classes & theme, and return a <Grid container direction="column">. 💅

4. Add Page Title 🏷️

Inside the grid, add <Grid item><Typography variant="h2">The Revolution</Typography></Grid>. Then in App.js, import Revolution and replace the dummy route to render your new component. 🚀

5. Style the Title ✍️

Give the title a special font.
<Typography className={classes.rowContainer} style={{ fontFamily: 'Pacifica' }} ...>
Add classes.rowContainer (copied from Custom Software page) and marginTop: '2m' for spacing. 🎉

6. Vision Section 🔭

Under the title, add:
<Grid item container direction="row" className={classes.rowContainer}>
• Left: <Grid item lg><img src={vision} alt="mountain through binoculars"/></Grid>
• Right: <Grid item container direction="column" lg> with an H4 subtitle and five body1 paragraphs (copy from design file). 📄

7. Share and Center Space ⚖️

On both grid items, add lg so they split evenly on large screens. Use alignItems="center" on the row container to center icon vertically. Give text & images a maxWidth: '40m' and image a marginRight: '5m'. 👍

8. Left‑Right Swap for Tech 💻

Copy the entire Vision row and paste under it. In the new block:
• Swap text to the left and animation on right.
• Change title to "Technology" and paste its paragraphs.
• Remove any align="right". 🎉

9. Add Lottie Animation 🎥

Import Lottie and your defaultOptions constant from Landing page. Replace <img> with:
<Lottie options={defaultOptions} isStopped />
Give it maxWidth: '40m' and margin: 0. Set row justify to flex-end. 🌟

10. Final Tweaks & Test 📱

Save and refresh! Check large and small screens. Items should share space on big, stack on mobile, and stay nicely centered with good spacing. Responsive design done! 🎉
