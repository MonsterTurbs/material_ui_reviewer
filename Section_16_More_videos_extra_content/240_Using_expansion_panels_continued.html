🎉 What Is an Expansion Panel?

• An Expansion Panel is like a little box that can open and close to show more details. 📦

• It has a “summary” part you always see (the label) and “details” you see when you click it. 🔍

• Use it when you want to hide extra info until the user needs it. 🙌

🔧 Key Components

ExpansionPanel – the overall container that can open/close.

ExpansionPanelSummary – the always-visible label area, with an “expand” arrow icon.

ExpansionPanelDetails – the hidden content that appears when opened.

ExpandMoreIcon – the little arrow icon you click to open/close. ↕️

✅ Uncontrolled vs. Controlled

• Uncontrolled panels open/close on their own when clicked.

• Controlled panels use React state (expanded) to decide when to open/close.

• Controlled panels let you make an “accordion” effect (only one open at a time). 🤹‍♂️

🛠️ Steps to Implement in Mobile Header

1. Import components:
ExpansionPanel, ExpansionPanelSummary,
ExpansionPanelDetails, ExpandMoreIcon
(and Grid for layout)

2. In your mobile menu mapping:
• When the route is “Services,” render an ExpansionPanel.
• Put the “Services” label inside ExpansionPanelSummary.
• Put service links inside ExpansionPanelDetails in a vertical Grid.

3. Handle selection:
• Keep track of both the main menu index and the service-tabs index.
• Update selectedIndex when a service link is clicked.

🎨 Styling Tweaks

• Remove default margins on the panel (.Mui-expanded).

• Match background color to your theme.

• Zero out extra padding in ExpansionPanelDetails.

• Add dividers and border styles for clearer separation.

• Color the expand icon to match your secondary theme color. 🧡

🔍 Inspect & Adjust

• Use browser DevTools to find unwanted margins/paddings.

• Target Material-UI’s classes (e.g. .MuiExpansionPanel-root) with classes overrides.

• Always align text/icons so they match the rest of your menu. ✔️

🤔 Why Use Expansion Panels?

• Great for hiding advanced options/details until needed.

• Keeps your UI simple and uncluttered on mobile. 📱

• Built-in accessibility and animations from Material UI. 🎉

🚀 Next Steps
