🔍 What is an Expansion Panel?

The Expansion Panel is a “collapsible” box that hides or shows content on demand. 📦

It helps keep your UI clean by showing just the title (summary) and letting you click to reveal details.

✨ Basic Structure

There are three main pieces:

  • ExpansionPanel: The outer wrapper.
  • ExpansionPanelSummary: Always-visible title area; click here to expand. 👆
  • ExpansionPanelDetails: Content that appears when expanded. 🎉
<ExpansionPanel>
  <ExpansionPanelSummary expandIcon=<ExpandMoreIcon />>
    Title Goes Here
  </ExpansionPanelSummary>
  <ExpansionPanelDetails>
    Hidden content goes here...
  </ExpansionPanelDetails>
</ExpansionPanel>

📊 Controlled vs Uncontrolled

Uncontrolled: Just click and it toggles itself automatically.

Controlled: You manage open/close in React state, like an accordion:

const [expanded, setExpanded] = useState(false);

<ExpansionPanel
  expanded={expanded === 'panel1'}
  onChange={(e, isOpen) => setExpanded(isOpen ? 'panel1' : false)}
>...
</ExpansionPanel>

⚙️ Customizing

🔹 expandIcon: Swap the arrow icon.

🔹 disableRipple, disabled, etc.: Use any usual MUI props.

🔹 Put any React/MUI components inside ExpansionPanelDetails—not just text!

🎛️ Extra: ExpansionPanelActions

Place action buttons at the bottom:

<ExpansionPanelActions>
  <Button>Cancel</Button>
  <Button color="primary">Save</Button>
</ExpansionPanelActions>

👍 Beginner Tips

  • Keep summaries short—like headings. 📋
  • Use icons to hint at expand/collapse (e.g. <ExpandMoreIcon/>).
  • Accordions: control panels with state so only one opens at a time.
  • Great for FAQs, settings, mobile menus, and any place to hide extra info! 🤫
