🏆 What Are Conversions?

Conversions (or “Goals”) are special actions users take—like sending a message or requesting an estimate—that you want to track in Google Analytics. 🎯

🔍 Why Track Conversions?

  • 📈 See how often key events happen (e.g., “estimate sent”).
  • 🌍 Know which users complete goals and where they come from.
  • 💡 Use data to improve your site and grow your business.

🛠️ Setting Up a Goal in GA

  1. 🔧 Go to Admin > View > Goals in Google Analytics.
  2. ➕ Click “New Goal” and choose a template (e.g., Contact Us).
  3. ✏️ Name your goal (e.g., message sent or estimate sent).
  4. 📊 Select “Event” as the goal type.
  5. 📋 Enter your event details: Category and Action.
  6. 💾 Save—your goal is now live! ✅

🎯 Example Goals

  • 💬 message sent: user clicked “Send Message.”
  • 🧮 estimate sent: user completed and submitted an estimate.

🚦 Testing Your Goals

  • 🔄 Use the Raw Data or Test View to see real-time conversions.
  • 🖱️ Perform the action (send message or estimate) on your site.
  • 👀 Go to Real-Time > Conversions in GA to confirm your goal fired.

🔍 Analyzing Goal Flow

Once data accumulates, use the Goal Flow report to see the pages users visited before converting—helping you optimize their journey. 🛣️

✅ Quick Recap

  • Goals = key actions you want users to complete. 🎯
  • Set up in GA under Admin > Goals, choose “Event” type.
  • Enter your event’s Category & Action, then save. 🛠️
  • Test in Real-Time > Conversions. 👀
  • Analyze Goal Flow to improve your website’s user path. 📊
